Personal Envoy
Jean Arnault (France) — appointed by SG Guterres. Nearly 40 years of international mediation; led UN missions in Afghanistan, Guatemala, Burundi, Georgia and Colombia. Will "be in contact with all parties" and examine civilian impact. Guterres: "time to stop climbing the escalation ladder." Neither the US nor Israel invited UN mediation — Arnault's leverage is limited but his appointment adds multilateral institutional legitimacy to ceasefire pressure.
UNOPS Task Force
Strait of Hormuz Task Force headed by Jorge Moreira da Silva (UNOPS Executive Director). Members: UNCTAD, IMO, ICC. Mandate: safe, orderly transit for humanitarian purposes — specifically fertiliser and raw materials. Analytical note: this is a humanitarian corridor mechanism, not a ceasefire instrument. It attempts to carve out protected transit within the blockade without requiring Iran to concede its Hormuz sovereignty demand.
UNSC Res. 2817
Resolution 2817 (12 March) — condemns Iran's "egregious attacks" against Gulf neighbours. Co-sponsored by 135 countries. Russia's rival draft condemning US-Israeli strikes was rejected. OHCHR experts separately condemned the Minab school strike (160+ students killed) and called for accountability for all parties.
